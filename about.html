来自社长的信
尊敬的各位指导老师，参会代表、主席：
我谨代表华东师范大学第二附属中学模拟联合国社团，2019年度华东师范大学第二附属中学模拟联合国暑期新手会（EAMUNC2019）组委欢迎各位。拟定于2019年7月28日到2019年7月31日的暑期新手会目前已经结束全部报名工作，我代表大会组委感谢各指导老师各位代表的辛勤付出。
模拟联合国起源于上个世纪50年代的美国高校，并迅速成为极受欢迎的学生学术活动。在活动中，学生们扮演不同国家或其它政治实体的外交代表，参与围绕国际上的热点问题召开的会议。代表们遵循议事规则，在会议主席团的主持下，通过演讲来阐述观点，为了“国家利益”辩论、磋商、游说。在这过程中，青年学生能够体验到联合国的运作机制、议事流程，接触到地缘政治、宏观经济等学科知识，并充分了解到国际时事，未来发展方向。
华东师大二附中模拟联合国社团（简称EFZMUN）成立于2004年，是全国最早组建并开展活动的中学模联社团之一，曾获得上海市明星社团、全国五佳高中模联社团。十五载春秋，二附中模联人走出了校园，越来越频繁地在上海各大高中，海内外顶尖高校，甚至是纽约联合国总部留下自己的足迹。一代又一代二附中模联人上传承着理想的荣光。
仲夏之约，青空之梦。卓然独立，越而胜己。愿各位能在这短暂的四天之中收获良多，感受模拟联合国独有的魅力与色彩。我在二附中静候各位的到来。
秘书长
胡君霖
会场简介

A
会场：联合国安全理事会
工作语言：中文
议题：委内瑞拉局势
会场介绍：2019年1月23日，在南美洲北部，暗流涌动，大规模的示威游行，反对派领袖胡安•瓜伊多的就职宣言，使得委内瑞拉不再平静。4月30日，部分军人发动的政变，反对派领袖胡安•瓜伊多的宣言，以及总统尼古拉斯•马杜罗及其所领导的政府做出的回应和反击再次将这个看似普普通通的国家推上了风口浪尖。在这场来势汹汹的政局动荡中，一只只幕后的大手，不断地走向台前，联合国安全理事会，也成为了这场动荡的焦点。支持还是反对？沉默还是爆发？代表们的选择，所影响的将不止是这一个国家。

B
会场：联合国环境规划署
工作语言：中文
议题：非洲野生动物保护问题
席位：35席 单代表制
2019年，人们社会面临的环境问题严峻，非洲地区的野生动物危机亦是迫在眉睫的危机。最近35年里，非洲犀牛97%的种群都已经消失，大象的数量减少了近90%，环境问题和人类活动密不可分，人类对于自然资源的掠夺式开发、毫无顾忌地向自然界排弃废物、过度的人口增长是造成环境问题的主要原因。解决环境问题，最重要的是依赖于各国的环境法和国际环境法。
联合国已经开始了对于非洲动物相关的环境法的制定，但是由于非洲的经济基础薄弱，民族宗教情况复杂，政治不稳定，环境法的实施往往滞后而难以落脚于实处。各国的博弈在环境保护中错综复杂。各位代表，你将如何兼顾国家利益和野生动物保护，从而达成多方的共识？

C
Committee: United Nations Human Rights Council
Topic: Protection of human rights of refugees in recipient countries
Language: English
Timeline: Year 2017
Delegation: Double Delegation
Conference Participants: 40 delegates
Introduction: Across the world, barriers are growing. With its origins in the aftermath of World War II, refugee problems emerged into sight of United Nations. Among them there is one problem which still needs solving and remains to be a challenging task, namely the protection of human rights of refugees in recipient countries. Human rights violations are a major factor in causing the flight of refugees as well as an obstacle to their safety and return home. Safeguarding human rights in recipient countries is therefore critical both for the prevention and for the solution of refugee problems. It is protection of human rights of refugees that brings about problems on how to guarantee three basic rights—rights to asylum, rights to freedom and rights to life, the implementation of which still requires improvement.
The establishment of asylum proceedings and refugee status determinations are left to each state party to develop. This has resulted in disparities among different states as governments craft asylum laws based on their different resources, national security concerns, and histories with forced migration movements. Despite differences at the national and regional levels, the overarching goal of the modern refugee regime is to provide protection to individuals forced to flee their homes because their countries are unwilling or unable to protect them.
Here at United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC), delegates are encouraged to have discussions about following questions: Who is responsible for upholding and protecting them? How can we hold them to account? How to challenge misconceptions, prejudices and discriminatory attitudes and behaviors towards refugees and asylum seekers? How to take action to protect the rights of people in need of international protection? How to improve the controlling international convention on refugee law on the basis that each country has its own characteristics?

D
Committee：The Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nation
Topic: International cooperation in the Elimination of Hunger, Food Safety and Malnutrition
Language: English
Conference Participants: 35 delegates (single delegation)
Introduction: After a prolonged decline, global hunger is again demonstrating its potential of uprising. The total supply of food is far from enough to meet the increasing demand. The situation is worse in developing countries in South America and most regions of Africa. In addition, the extreme disastrous climate change is bringing challenges to countries with little advanced technology to diminish the damage. However, issues like child overweight, adult obesity and malnutrition are more frequently and rampantly corroding people’s health in developed regions; Meanwhile the simultaneous existence of over-nutrition and under-nutrition has become a common phenomenon in developing countries. There is an urgent call for the global mobilization in the eradication of the aforementioned severe issues. The United Nations has established frameworks and projects including the Millennium Development Goals and Food, Zero Huger Challenge which are making much progress in reducing hunger and malnutrition. International cooperation between countries, regions and organizations is vital in promoting trans-regional financial and technical support, improving the current agriculture system and reaching agreements on the problems. Moreover, small holders and local indigenous people’s productivity and income are having impact on the food supply chain. To fully resolve the existing challenges and to fulfill the ambitious 2030 Sustainable Development Goal agenda, further and deeper elaboration of the current cooperation mechanisms ought to be carried out.
